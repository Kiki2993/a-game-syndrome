Synopsis

A used game cartridge. A child who bought it because it was cheap. A game called Phantasmagoria — designed to read players' deepest fears and never stop reading.

What no one knew was that something had already been inside the cartridge long before ChromaVerse Studios ever shipped it. Something with no form, no name, no presence in any information system — only the absence of data where data should be.

Years pass. The cartridge changes hands. The fear deepens. And when the right hands return — in a boarding house room in Tangerang, on a night of academic frustration and an impulsive, exhausted click — what had been waiting with infinite patience finally finds its opening.

A Game Syndrome is a novel about AUP: An Unknown Player. An entity not registered in the information system of any layer, any dimension, any cosmology. Not a monster. Not a god. A condition — one whose only property is the absence of properties, and whose only consequence is everything.

About the Author

Kibe is the author name behind A Game Syndrome — an original English-language cosmic horror and meta-fiction novel. The novel is also available on Royal Road and Inkitt.

All worldbuilding concepts in this novel — the cosmological layer system, TCS, the entity designated AUP, and the mechanism of Cognitive Fear Signature — originated from the author. The prose exists to document what was already there before it was written.
